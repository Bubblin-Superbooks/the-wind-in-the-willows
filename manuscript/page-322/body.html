Then he took to giggling in a shy, self-conscious manner. At last he got up, locked the door, drew the curtains across the windows, collected all the chairs in the room and arranged them in a semicircle, and took up his position in front of them, swelling visibly. Then he bowed, coughed twice, and, letting himself go, with uplifted voice he sang, to the enraptured audience that his imagination so clearly saw:

TOAD'S LAST LITTLE SONG

The Toad—came—home! 
There was panic in the parlours
and howling in the halls, 
There was crying in the cow-sheds
